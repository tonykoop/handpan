# Image-Gen Prompts

Concept image only; not fabrication authority. Do not include cut-ready dimensions, hole locations, tone-field geometry, dimple shapes, or toolpaths. Tone-field shapes are tuner-adjusted and must never be drawn as final.

Photorealistic 3/4 product photograph of a finished D Kurd handpan: a smooth lenticular UFO-shaped steel hand drum formed from two domed sheet-steel shells joined at a clean rim seam, a single convex central Ding dome at the top center surrounded by a ring of eight gently hammered oval tone fields, each tone field a softly dimpled depression with a subtle raised note interface, the whole satin-grey nitrided steel surface showing faint hammer-worked texture and a warm dark patina, hand-playable lap-scale cues, brushed metal catching soft directional studio light, clean neutral studio background, soft directional studio light, 3/4 angle, ~50mm lens, high detail, photorealistic, no text labels.

## Detail Prompt

Photorealistic macro close-up of the signature mechanism: the central convex Ding dome and two neighboring hammered oval tone fields on the steel handpan top shell, each tone field a smooth shallow dimpled depression worked into the curved steel with soft shoulder transitions and subtle concentric hammer-forming marks, satin nitrided grey-steel surface with faint patina, raking light revealing the gentle curvature and worked texture of the shell, the rim seam blurred at the frame edge, high detail, no measurements.

## Negative / Avoid

No text, logos, watermarks, or fake maker marks; no dimension callouts; no cut tongues, slots, or machined tone-field contours (tongueless tuned-shell drum, tuner-adjusted fields); anatomically plausible hands if a player is shown.
