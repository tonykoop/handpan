# Handpan — Starter Songbook

Your handpan is tuned to **D Kurd** (D natural minor / Aeolian):

```
Ding: D3 (center, lower dome)
Tone fields (arranged in a circle on the upper dome):
  A3 — Bb3 — C4 — D4 — E4 — F4 — G4 — A4
```

The handpan is played with bare hands — thumbs, fingertips, and the heel of the hand.
Never use mallets, picks, or hard objects on the handpan.

## Difficulty key
- ★☆☆ — Beginner: ding + adjacent fields, slow patterns
- ★★☆ — Intermediate: full field circle, simple melodies
- ★★★ — Advanced: bass tones, taks, cross-handed patterns

## Stroke vocabulary
| Stroke | Hand | Sound |
|--------|------|-------|
| **Ding** | Thumb or ring finger on ding dome | Deep, resonant center tone |
| **Tone** | Fingertip on outer edge of tone field | Clear, singing pitch |
| **Slap** | Flat finger, quick release | Short, muted "tak" click |
| **Bass** | Hand presses center of tone field then lifts | Muffled, bass-register version of field |

## Safety note
The handpan surface is finished steel. Keep fingernails trimmed. Do not use rings
or jewelry while playing. After playing, wipe the surface with a dry cloth and
apply a small amount of protective oil (phoenix oil or similar) monthly.
