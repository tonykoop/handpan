# Ding Meditation ★☆☆

Free-time, no rhythm. This is a listening exercise, not a melody.

## Pattern 1: Ding alone
Strike the ding with your dominant thumb. Let it ring completely to silence.
Wait 3 breaths. Repeat 8 times.

## Pattern 2: Ding + one field
Strike the ding. While it rings, gently add one tone field.
Start with the A3 field (a perfect fifth). Then try Bb3 (minor 6th) — hear the tension.
Then D4 (an octave) — hear the resolution.

## Pattern 3: Field circle
Walk slowly around the field circle: A3 → Bb3 → C4 → D4 → E4 → F4 → G4 → A4 → A3.
No rhythm — just tone by tone, one breath per strike.

## Pattern 4: Ding as pulse
Use the ding as a slow heartbeat (one stroke every 2 seconds).
Above it, improvise freely on the tone fields between ding strokes.

**Duration:** 10–15 minutes. This prepares the hands, ears, and attention for playing.
